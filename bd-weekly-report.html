Action Plan
3–5 specific actions based on W17 data · Updated Apr 29, 2026
1
🚫 FIX
Gate or disable the “Popup – Blacklist Countries” source
17 of 77 contacts this week (22.1%) came in as spam through this source — the highest single waste category. Either add a stricter country-block on the popup trigger, require a qualifying field before submission, or redirect users from blacklisted countries to a “coming soon” page. This alone could raise the valid rate from 11.7% to ~14%+ immediately.
2
⛔ STOP / REDUCE
Pull back on bulk outbound batches until targeting is improved
Outbound generated 41 contacts, 5 valid leads, and zero connections this week. Every outbound valid lead said “Not Interested”. Last week 93 outbound contacts produced the same connect rate (0%). This is a volume play with no pipeline return. Pause bulk outbound, audit the targeting list, and only resume with tighter ICP criteria (company size, industry, geography).
3
▲ DOUBLE DOWN
Fast-track Geneva MSAI and Vivek Sadh — both are Valid + BD Qualified
Two inbound leads created this week are Valid and have a “BD Qualified” status but are not yet Connected. Geneva MSAI (Medical Staff AI, Apr 28) and Vivek Sadh (Jellyfish Technologies, Apr 27) should be contacted today. Inbound valid leads that aren’t followed up within 48 hours typically go cold — both are in the window right now.
4
🔧 IMPROVE
Add a jobseeker filter to the Company Page Inquiry form
8 jobseekers (10.4% of leads) entered through Company Page Inquiry this week. Add a single qualifying question to the LinkedIn / contact page form: “Are you looking to hire outsourced staff, or are you looking for a job?” Route the latter to a careers page. This is a cheap fix that removes a recurring noise source.
5
📋 TRACK
Reactivate SP channel — it had a 100% connect rate in W16
W16 had 1 SP-tagged lead (Daniel Coal) with Valid + Connected status — 100% connect rate. W17 had zero SP leads. This is either a tracking gap or a channel that’s gone quiet. Confirm with the SP team whether the source is active, and if so, why no BD-tagged leads came through this week. SP leads are the highest-quality leads in the data; this channel should be prioritized.
Pipeline Notes
Patterns, early indicators, and context · Week 17
PixelAscend deal created from BD lead (Eddie Toth, Apr 23) — 7-day cycle from contact creation to deal. This is the fastest funnel completion this period and matches the profile of a strong inbound lead: Valid validity, direct connection made, deal opened as “Sales Closer” role. Replicate this intake pattern.
27 total deals created this week — 4 in the BD pipeline (PixelAscend, cubexuk.com, Vista Film, DC Center LLC) and 23 in Client Services. CS pipeline deal flow more than doubled vs W16. This may reflect improved qualifying by the CS team or a backlog of SP companies being onboarded.
W16 deal count anomaly — Prior week showed 176 total deals, but ~163 were a bulk SP directory import created on Apr 16–17 (all in early/holding stage). The W16 comparison baseline used in this report is 13 normal deals, not 176. This import should be reviewed to ensure these companies are being actively worked, not just added as database entries.
Early indicator — Inbound quality is consistent — Both W16 and W17 produced exactly 2 connected leads from inbound sources, despite W17 having more inbound volume (35 vs 26). Valid→Connected rate improved (22.2% vs 11.8%). Inbound is the stable engine; outbound is noise at current targeting quality.
Risk — 3 unscored outbound contacts — James Travis (Singletrack), Nicholas Trueb, and Alex Grechishkin (Bricklane.com) have no lead_validity set in HubSpot despite being created Apr 22–27. All are tagged “BD Lead;OA Outbound.” These need manual review or an automated validity rule to avoid gaps in funnel reporting.
